## Extended launch blurb (for outlets wanting more detail)

> Flute Hero was built around a specific gap: private flute lessons and school band
> rehearsals happen once or twice a week, but a beginner needs to practise the other six
> days — and most parents of flute students aren't flute players themselves, so they
> can't tell if their child is playing a note correctly.
>
> The app listens to a child play through the device's microphone and gives real-time
> feedback — in tune, close, or not yet — using on-device pitch detection. Nothing is
> recorded, uploaded, or stored; there is no account, no login, and no chat features.
> Progress (levels completed, notes mastered, streaks, badges) is stored only in the
> browser on the device being used.
>
> The curriculum spans 8 levels — First Breath, Finding My Voice, Middle Path, Reaching
> Higher, Taking Flight, Sharps & Flats, Filling the Gaps, and Sky High — moving from a
> student's first three notes to the full chromatic range used in early band and
> orchestra repertoire, with real playable songs at every stage. A Foundations track
> covers note reading, embouchure, and tonguing for students who have never held a flute.
>
> Pricing is a single $24.99 payment with no subscription: Level 1 remains free forever,
> and every new user receives a 10-day trial with the complete curriculum unlocked before
> deciding whether to buy.
