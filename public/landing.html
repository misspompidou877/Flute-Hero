Questions parents ask

Does my child need their own flute?
Yes — Flute Hero teaches a real acoustic flute. Any standard student flute works. The app listens to the actual instrument; there's no plastic recorder or on-screen keyboard substitute.
What device and internet do we need?
Any modern iPad, iPhone or Android tablet with a browser and a microphone. You need internet to load it the first time, but the pitch analysis itself runs entirely on the device.
How much time does it take?
Short, daily practice is the whole idea — about 10 minutes a day is plenty. Once you've set it up the first time, your child can open it and practise on their own; no parent needs to sit in.
Does it work in a noisy house?
It listens for the clear, sustained tone of the flute, so it copes well with normal household hum. Loud TV or a sibling right beside the tablet can throw it off — a quiet-ish corner gives the cleanest feedback, just like it would for a teacher in the room.
Is my child recorded?
No. The microphone is used only to detect pitch in real time. Audio is never saved, never uploaded and never sent anywhere. Nothing leaves the tablet.
What if they want to quit?
Level 1 is free forever, so you can see if it clicks before paying anything. If you do unlock everything, it's a one-time price — there's no subscription quietly billing you after they've moved on. And because it's built like a game — streaks, levels and an instant green light when they nail a note — most kids actually want to come back.
What's the right age?
Flute Hero is designed for children roughly 7–12, sized for small hands on a tablet propped on a music stand. Slightly older beginners enjoy it too.
Does it replace a teacher?
It's a brilliant between-lessons practice partner, not a replacement for a great teacher. It makes daily practice count by catching tuning slips a busy parent can't — which teachers love.
Not another subscription?
Correct — no subscription. Level 1 is free, and unlocking the rest is a single $24.99 payment that includes every future update. That's it.
What happens when the 10-day trial ends?
Nothing bad. No card is taken to start the trial, so nothing is ever charged. The app simply returns to the free Level 1 — your child keeps everything they reached there — and you can unlock the rest whenever you're ready.
